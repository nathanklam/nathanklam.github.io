15lb Combat Robot: “Master Oogway”

Overview

  • Budget $1000
  • Weapon Asymmetric dual-disk AR500 steel vertical spinner; 1.8 kJ of energy after 2 seconds of spin-up.
  • Weapon Drive PROPDRIVE v2 4258 500KV, 1.4:1 belt reduction
  • Drive PROPDRIVE v2 3536 910KV; 16:1 gear reduction, belted 4-wheel drive
  • Armor Angled aluminum and steel plates optimized for impact deflection
  • Design Priorities Modularity, durability, manufacturability, rapid serviceability

Design Process

As part of the 2nd semester at Combat Robotics at Berkeley, my teammates and I were tasked with designing a 15 lb robot, which greatly expanded upon the possibilities available compared to our first projects: the 1 lb plastic antweights. mention the ideas from our meetings and how that shows up as the image desired a 4wd and thus resulted in a more square shape, a dual disk to differentiate ourselves from an previous bot from the club, belted drive and weapon (no hub motor) and an angled exterior to deflect weapon impacts, which we presented during our first design review

Due to our limited budget of $1000 dollars and the capabilities student organization's manufacturing sponsors, we were essentially constrained to designing for 2D manufacturing. The only exception to this was the hub for the dual disks, which itself costed a few hundred due to the complex geometry. Regardless, everything had to be flat plates.
