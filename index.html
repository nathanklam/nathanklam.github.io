About Me

I am a second-year undergraduate student majoring in Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. My interests lie in mechanical design, manufacturing, and prototyping, and how these intersect with electronics.

My project work emphasizes design-for-manufacturing, modularity, and learning through iterative testing while accounting for weight, manufacturing, and budgetary constraints. This is especially evident for my projects regarding combat robotics, where designs are forced to prove themselves quickly.

"Skadoosh" (Winter 2026-), "Eggsterminator" (Fall 2024)

Eggsterminator was a 1 lb plastic combat robot focused on survivability through angled geometry and print-oriented design, earning Most Destructive in an internal competition. Skadoosh builds on this foundation with an improved weapon system and experimental front-wedge geometry to add controlled engagement and opponent manipulation capabilities. The second iteration went back to conventional design, winning several matches. The third iteration is in development.

Coursework Projects

Culinary Bot (Spring 2026)

This project presents a robotic culinary plating assistant that uses a UR7e arm with an attached camera to capture a completed dish from multiple viewpoints and reconstruct a 3D model of the plated arrangement. The system then identifies food items, estimates their poses relative to varying frames, and generates a pick-and-place plan to recreate the dish.

Course: MEC106A - Introduction to Robotics

Tools/Skills: ROS2 · YOLO · Python · HTML · CSS

Composite Armor in Combat Robotics Applications (Spring 2026)

A simulation project, investigating the behavior of composites under medium-velocity impacts (up to 110 m/s) with non-linear trajectories and high mass. The goal is to explore and possibly develop alternatives to aluminum 6061 and steel for armor applications in combat robotics.

Course: ME127 - Introduction to Composite Materials

Tools/Skills: Ansys · LS-DYNA · Fusion 360 · LaTeX
